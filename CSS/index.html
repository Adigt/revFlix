Summary

Directed by Luis Valdez, La Bamba is a biographical film, loosely based on the real life of a teenage Mexican migrant, Ritchie Valens who achieved an overnight Rock 'n' Roll success in 1958, as one of the first Latinos in the industry. From living in refugee camps in the country to trailer van in the city, from playing at a garage party to going on tours with the very best, from nothingness to living the American Dream, the psth is all-set for 17-year-old Ritchie Valens.

Plot Elements

  • Sibling Rivalry

    • The ups and downs in the relationship between two brothers with entirely different personalities. Ritchie's elder brother tries to provide for him yet messes up things. However, whenever the need arises Ritchie relies on him as his best friend and companion. The overnight success of Ritchie's music career while him being the black sheep of the family has adverse effects on his mind. The loving brother gets jealous and messes up in a public gathering, however Ritchie forgives him afterwards.

  • Art before Commercial success

    • Back in the late 50's when people struggled with their ethnicity in Hollywood, Ritchie Valens takes a bold move with his rock 'n' roll version of La Bamba a Spanish folk song. While he did accept the stage name given by his manager to hide his ethnicity, he did not let it hamper the making of this very song regarded as the second best of his career.

  • Dreams and Nightmares

    • While Ritchie Valens achieves his rock 'n' roll dream overnight, he also suffers from the nightmares of a plane crash which haunts him since his early days.
    In February 1959, while on their Chicago tour, their private plane crashes north of Clear Lake claimed the lives of rock 'n' roll musicians Buddy Holly, Ritchie Valens, J. P. "The Big Bopper" Richardson and pilot Roger Peterson.
    Fate catches up with the rising sensation.
